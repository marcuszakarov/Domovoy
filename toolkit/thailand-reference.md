# Thailand Real Estate — Quick Reference

## Foreign Ownership Rules (Critical Knowledge)

### Condominiums
- Foreigners **CAN own freehold** (outright ownership)
- **49% foreign quota** per building — once it's full, no more foreign buyers
- Always verify the foreign quota with the juristic office BEFORE committing
- Ownership registered at the **Land Department office**

### Land & Houses (Villas, Townhouses)
- Foreigners **CANNOT own land** in Thailand
- **Legal structures for houses**:

| Structure | How it Works | Risk Level |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| **Leasehold** | 30-year lease, renewable (but renewal not guaranteed by law) | Medium |
| **Thai company** | Set up a Thai Co. Ltd. to hold the land (common but gray area) | Higher |
| **Thai spouse** | Land in Thai spouse's name | Depends on relationship |
| **BOI / Treaty of Amity** | Special investment schemes (US citizens + certain businesses) | Complex |
| **Usufruct** | Right to use land for life (registered at Land Office) | Medium |
| **Superficies** | Right to own buildings on someone else's land | Medium |

### Recommended Approach for Domovoy Clients
- **Condos**: Straightforward freehold — safest option
- **Villas**: 30-year leasehold + option to renew is the most common legitimate structure
- **Always**: Engage a Thai property lawyer (not the seller's lawyer)

## Title Deeds — What to Check

### Types of Title Deed (from strongest to weakest)

| Type | Thai Name | Strength | Notes |
|------|-----------|----------|-------|
| **Chanote** (Nor Sor 4 Jor) | โฉนด | Strongest | Full private ownership, GPS-surveyed boundaries. **This is what you want.** |
| **Nor Sor 3 Gor** | น.ส.3 ก. | Good | Confirmed possession, can be upgraded to Chanote |
| **Nor Sor 3** | น.ส.3 | Medium | Possession right, 30-day posting required for sale |
| **Sor Kor 1** | ส.ค.1 | Weak | Notification of possession only, not a full title |
| **Por Bor Tor 5** | ภ.บ.ท.5 | Tax doc only | NOT a title deed — just proves tax was paid on land |

### Red Flags
- Seller can't produce original Chanote (it's at the bank = still mortgaged)
- Title deed doesn't match the seller's ID
- Land area on deed doesn't match actual land (encroachment)
- Property is on Sor Kor 1 or has no proper title
- Recent transfers (could indicate rushed sale to avoid legal issues)
- Land is in a **forest reserve zone** — illegal to build, impossible to own
- Land is in a **national park buffer zone** — restricted development

### Verification
- Go to the **local Land Office** (Samnak Ngan Thi Din) with the seller
- Request a title deed search — confirms ownership, mortgages, encumbrances
- Costs: ~100-500 THB for a search
- Takes: 1-3 business days
- **Always do this. No exceptions.**

## Property Taxes & Fees

### On Purchase
| Fee | Rate | Paid By |
|-----|------|---------|
| Transfer fee | 2% of appraised value | Usually split 50/50 |
| Stamp duty | 0.5% of appraised or sale price (whichever is higher) | Seller |
| Specific business tax | 3.3% (if held < 5 years, replaces stamp duty) | Seller |
| Withholding tax | Progressive rate (individuals) / 1% (companies) | Seller |

### Annual
| Tax | Rate | Notes |
|-----|------|-------|
| Land & Building Tax | 0.02%-0.1% of appraised value | Residential rates are low |
| Common area fee | Varies by condo/project | Monthly |

### Rental Income Tax (If your clients rent out)
- **Personal income tax**: Progressive rate 0-35%
- **Withholding tax on rent**: Tenant should withhold 5% at source (if tenant is a company)
- Most small landlords: handled via annual tax filing
- **Tip**: Many expats don't know about Thai rental income tax — flag it

## Phuket Zones — Agent's Quick Guide

### Premium / International Family Zones

**Koh Kaew / British International School area**
- Closest to BISP (British International School Phuket)
- Mix of villas and housing estates
- Rent range: 35,000-120,000 THB/mo
- Feel: suburban, green, family-oriented

**Thalang / UWC / Thanyapura area**
- Near UWC Thailand and Thanyapura Sports Centre
- Large plots, nature, slightly remote
- Rent range: 30,000-100,000 THB/mo
- Feel: countryside, sporty, international

**Cherng Talay / Bang Tao / Laguna area**
- Laguna Phuket complex, beach access
- Premium villas, some condos
- Rent range: 40,000-250,000+ THB/mo
- Feel: resort-style living, expat hub

**Kathu / QSI / Kajonkiet area**
- Central, good access to everywhere
- Mix of condos and villas, more local feel
- Rent range: 15,000-60,000 THB/mo
- Feel: practical, less touristy, real Phuket

**Chalong / Rawai / South area**
- Popular with Europeans and Russians
- Mix of everything
- Rent range: 15,000-80,000 THB/mo
- Feel: laid-back, restaurants, yacht clubs

**Wichit / Phuket Town area**
- Most local, most authentic
- Condos and townhouses
- Rent range: 10,000-40,000 THB/mo
- Feel: real Thai town, street food, markets

## Common Due Diligence Checklist

Before recommending any property to a Domovoy family:

### Legal
- [ ] Chanote title deed verified at Land Office
- [ ] No encumbrances or mortgages
- [ ] Seller identity matches title deed
- [ ] Foreign quota available (condos)
- [ ] Lease agreement reviewed by independent lawyer (leasehold)
- [ ] EIA (Environmental Impact Assessment) compliant if applicable
- [ ] Building permit valid and matches actual construction
- [ ] No ongoing legal disputes

### Physical
- [ ] Property inspected in person (use inspection checklist)
- [ ] Structural condition acceptable
- [ ] All utilities functional (water, electric, internet)
- [ ] Flood risk assessed (Phuket has monsoon flooding in certain areas)
- [ ] Access road quality checked (some Phuket sois are brutal in rainy season)
- [ ] Noise assessment (construction, nightlife, airport flight path)

### Practical for Families
- [ ] Distance to school measured (drive in traffic, not Google estimate)
- [ ] School bus route available?
- [ ] Nearest hospital (Bangkok Hospital Phuket, Siriroj, Mission Hospital)
- [ ] Nearest supermarket (Villa Market, Tops, Makro)
- [ ] Internet speed tested (important for remote-working parents)
- [ ] Mobile signal strength checked (all 3 carriers: AIS, True, DTAC)

## Seasonal Considerations

| Season | Months | Impact on Property |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **High Season** | Nov - Apr | Prices peak, availability lowest, best weather for viewings |
| **Green/Low Season** | May - Oct | Better negotiation, but rain makes viewings harder. **Test properties in rain to see drainage.** |
| **Monsoon Peak** | Sep - Oct | Heaviest rain. Perfect time to check for leaks, flooding, road access |
| **Chinese New Year** | Jan/Feb | Surge in Chinese buyer interest |
| **School Start** | Aug/Sep | Peak demand from relocating families (your Domovoy sweet spot) |
