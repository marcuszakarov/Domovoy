# Ray-Ban Meta 2 — Real Estate Workflows

## Why Ray-Ban Meta 2 for Real Estate

Your glasses are your secret weapon. While other agents fumble with phones, tripods, and notebooks, you walk through properties naturally, capturing everything hands-free. Clients see a professional having a conversation, not someone buried in a device.

---

## Core Workflows

### 1. Hands-Free Property Walkthrough Video

**When**: Every property viewing (build your video library)

**How**:
1. Say **"Hey Meta, start recording"** as you approach the property
2. Walk through naturally — the camera is at eye level, exactly as a buyer would see it
3. **Narrate** as you walk: "Entering the living area, notice the double-height ceiling and the sea view through the floor-to-ceiling windows"
4. To capture a photo at any moment: **"Hey Meta, take a photo"**
5. Say **"Hey Meta, stop recording"** when done
6. Video syncs to Meta View app on iPhone → then to cloud

**Pro Tips**:
- Look where you want the viewer to look — your head direction IS the camera direction
- **Pause and hold your gaze** for 3-5 seconds on key features (pool, view, kitchen island)
- Move your head slowly — quick movements create nauseating footage
- The glasses mic picks up your voice clearly, even in windy Thai outdoor areas
- **Duration**: Keep walkthroughs under 5 minutes (attention span of remote buyers)

### 2. AI-Powered Property Analysis

**When**: During inspections, when you spot something you need to identify

**Voice Commands**:
- **"Hey Meta, what kind of wood is this?"** — Point your gaze at flooring/cabinetry
- **"Hey Meta, what brand is this air conditioning unit?"** — Look at the AC
- **"Hey Meta, does this look like water damage?"** — Look at a ceiling stain
- **"Hey Meta, what type of stone is this countertop?"** — Look at surfaces
- **"Hey Meta, what style of architecture is this?"** — Exterior assessment

**Real Estate Use Cases**:
| Question to Ask | Why It Matters |
|----------------|---------------|
| "What material is this?" | Identify premium vs. cheap finishes |
| "What brand is this?" | Appliance and fixture valuation |
| "Does this look damaged?" | Document defects objectively |
| "What plant is this?" | Garden/landscaping knowledge |
| "What type of pool is this?" | Infinity, overflow, plunge — terminology matters |
| "Translate this sign" | Thai signage at properties, neighborhoods |

### 3. Live Property Viewing for Remote Clients

**When**: International families can't fly to Phuket yet (your Domovoy core audience)

**Setup**:
1. Start a **WhatsApp video call** or **Instagram Live** through the glasses
2. The client sees exactly what you see — first-person property tour
3. They can ask you to look at specific things in real-time
4. You stay hands-free to open doors, flip switches, test taps

**Script**:
```
"Hi [Family Name], I'm here at the property on [Street/Soi].
Let me walk you through it as if you were here with me.
Tell me anytime you want me to look at something more closely.
Let's start from the entrance..."
```

**Killer Feature**: The client feels like THEY are walking through the property. This builds trust with overseas families relocating to Phuket far better than edited photos.

### 4. Voice Notes During Inspections

**When**: During every property visit — capture thoughts without breaking stride

**How**: **"Hey Meta, take a note"** → speak your observation

**What to Note**:
- "North-facing living room, will get afternoon shade — good for Thai heat"
- "Kitchen is undersized for the price point, would need renovation"
- "Pool pump sounds rough, probably needs servicing within 6 months"
- "Neighbor construction visible from master bedroom — check building permits"
- "This would be perfect for the Anderson family — 3 kids, near Headstart"
- "Title: check if this is Chanote or Nor Sor 3 Gor"

Notes sync to your phone and can be transcribed automatically.

### 5. Translation Assistance

**When**: Meeting Thai landlords, reading Thai documents, neighborhood signs

**How**:
- **"Hey Meta, translate this to English"** — while looking at Thai text
- **"Hey Meta, how do I say 'Can I see the title deed?' in Thai"**
- **"Hey Meta, what does this sign say?"** — look at Thai signage

**Common Phrases to Practice**:
| English | Thai (phonetic) |
|---------|----------------|
| How much per month? | Kee baht tor deuan? |
| Can I see the Chanote? | Kor doo Chanote dai mai? |
| When is this available? | Wang meu a meu rai? |
| Is the price negotiable? | Lor ra-ka dai mai? |
| Who is the owner? | Jao kong pen krai? |

## Best Practices

### Battery Management
- **Full charge**: ~4 hours of mixed use
- **Heavy video**: ~2.5 hours
- **Between viewings**: Put them in the charging case (gives ~2 extra full charges)
- **Rule**: Charge during lunch, charge during drives (keep case in car)

### When to Use Glasses vs. iPhone

| Situation | Use Ray-Ban Meta 2 | Use iPhone 17 Pro |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Property walkthrough video | Yes — natural POV | For stabilized cinematic shots |
| Quick photos | Yes — no phone fumbling | For 48MP detail/hero shots |
| Client live viewing | Yes — first-person immersion | For sending edited photos |
| Voice notes | Yes — hands-free | For longer dictation |
| Room measurements | No | Yes — LiDAR |
| Floor plans | No | Yes — Polycam/RoomPlan |
| Night/low light photos | No | Yes — Night mode |
| Detail/macro shots | No | Yes — 5x zoom, macro |

### Etiquette in Thailand
- **Always mention** you're wearing smart glasses with a camera when entering a property
- Thai culture values **respect and privacy** — don't record without permission
- During formal meetings with landlords, consider **removing the glasses** (traditional respect)
- When recording: **"Kor a-nu-yaat tai video dai mai krap/ka?"** (May I take video?)
- **Temples and sacred areas**: Remove or turn off — never record

### Video Quality Tips
- Clean the lenses before every viewing (Thai humidity causes smudges)
- Ray-Ban Meta 2 shoots at 1080p — good for walkthroughs, not for hero content
- Best in natural daylight — indoor performance is decent but not iPhone-level
- The ultra-wide angle captures more of each room than you'd expect

## Workflow: Viewing Day with Ray-Ban Meta 2

```
Morning:
  Charge glasses + case to 100%
  Clean lenses
  Sync Meta View app → clear old media for storage

At Property:
  "Hey Meta, start recording" → walk the exterior
  "Hey Meta, take a photo" → front facade, signage, street
  Enter property → continue recording
  Narrate naturally as you explore
  "Hey Meta, take a note" → capture observations
  Use AI: "What material is this flooring?"
  "Hey Meta, stop recording"

  Switch to iPhone:
  → LiDAR scan (Polycam) each room
  → 48MP hero shots of best features
  → Detail shots (5x zoom: roof, ceiling, fixtures)

Post-Viewing:
  Open Meta View app → videos and photos sync
  Open Notes → review transcribed voice notes
  All media in one place for the listing
```
